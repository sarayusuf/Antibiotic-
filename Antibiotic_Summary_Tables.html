2 · Penicillins

Class-wide properties: Cell wall inhibitors (bactericidal) · Excreted renally (dose-adjust in renal impairment) · Cross placenta → Pregnancy Category B (except Imipenem) · Inactive against atypical bacteria · Do NOT combine with bacteriostatic agents · May cause allergy, GI upset, interstitial nephritis, bone marrow suppression (with prolonged use), seizures (with accumulation)

8 · Tetracyclines & Glycylclines

13a · Pregnancy Safety Summary
Category B — Generally SAFE Category C — Use ONLY if benefit outweighs risk Category D — CONTRAINDICATED
  • All Penicillins
  • All Cephalosporins (1st–5th gen)
  • Meropenem, Doripenem, Ertapenem, Aztreonam
  • Daptomycin
  • Azithromycin, Erythromycin, Spiramycin
  • Clindamycin

  • Imipenem/Cilastatin ← only beta-lactam Cat C
  • Vancomycin (IV route)
  • Clarithromycin
  • Chloramphenicol
  • Lincomycin
  • Linezolid
  • All Aminoglycosides
  • All Tetracyclines (including Tigecycline)
  • All Quinolones
  • Cotrimoxazole / Sulfonamides
Special pregnancy considerations: Spiramycin is used for toxoplasmosis in pregnancy. Benzathine penicillin is the treatment for syphilis in pregnancy (2.4 MU IM). Silver sulfadiazine topically is acceptable for burns. Metronidazole: avoid in 1st trimester if possible.
